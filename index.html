The method

Nothing new. That is the point.

For fourteen centuries the Quran has been memorised the same way: hear it from a reciter, repeat it until it settles, then recite it back unseen. This tool only removes the friction.

```
تَلَقِّيTalaqqi

Receive it from a reciter

Choose from nine recitations — classical voices like Husary's Mu'allim teaching recording, or contemporary ones like Maher al-Muaiqly — and let the correct sound become the memory itself.

تَكْرَارTakrar

Repeat each ayah until it holds

The loop engine plays one ayah three, five, seven or ten times before moving to the next — then cycles the whole passage again. The old counting method, counted for you.

حِفْظHifz

Veil the page and recite

One tap blurs the ink while the page stays in front of you. Stuck on a single ayah? Tap it to peek — the way a teacher lets you glance at one word, never the whole page.
